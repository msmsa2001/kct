KNOW ABOUT US

"Nurturing Hope, Changing Lives: Khidmat Charitable Trust's Compassionate Mission "

Khidmat Charitable Trust (KCT) is a Non-Government Organization (NGO) registered under the Government of India’s Public Trust Registration Act of 1950 on 12 December 2001.

Based in Mumbai, KCT’s registration no is E19852. In 2001, by the grace of Almighty Allah, we established our start-up with the key objective of providing every possible medical aid to the underprivileged and deserving section of our society. Our aim is to provide them moral and economical support during the treatment of a critical illness.

ACCESSING AID :

A Step-by-Step Guide for Beneficiaries

Guiding Beneficiaries: From Application to Approval, A
Comprehensive Overview of Accessing Aid from Khidmat Charitable
Trust.

01

In-person meeting

Anyone in need of any assistance in a medical emergency has to apply in writing, explaining his or her needs and circumstances in detail. The Trust vets all the applications carefully and then an executive from the Khidmat Charitable Trust schedule a face-to face meeting with the applicant. During this interaction, the KCT executive takes a full account of the individual's case, tries to understand their problems, economic and social conditions.

02

Verification

KCT's Medical Officer then examines the medical procedure, while a pharmacist screens the medicine prescription and also gives an estimate of the approximate cost involved for medicines, procedures, etc.

03

Filling of Forms

Once the background check is complete and the executive is satisfied with the authenticity of the case, the applicant is asked to fill-out a 'Khidmat Enrollment' form, along with a photo, identity proof such as PAN or Aadhaar card. In the case of medical assistance, a doctor's prescription is also mandatory.

04

Sanction and Deed

Lastly, the Trustees scan eac and every form carefully and give their final approval for prompt action. The medicines are delivered to the location with open seals to avoid reselling. No cash, only cheque policy is adopted for monetary transactions.
