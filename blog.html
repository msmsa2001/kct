Dialysis Centre

Yearly, around 2,20,000 patients in India are diagnosed with end-stage renal disease (ESRD). Every year, there is an added chunk of 34 million dialysis sessions added to the existing pool of patients. Nearly 70% of this target population of patients are not able to afford or continue with the recurring treatment due to fiscal limitations or untimely death. KCT Remedy

The KCT Trust’s first measure was to spread more awareness in our society about the urgent need to increase capacity for dialysis procedure. Since this was not available with the government hospitals and private hospitals were out of bounds for poor patients, KCT Trustees eventually decided to set-up their own center for provision of regular dialysis sessions. This Centre also distributes medicine, gives injections and provides for the patients’ ration needs through our different programs. At the center, we have a team that helps them understand how they can avail which government schemes to lessen their disease burden with financial aid.


Tasks & Structure

  • Dialysis centre is occupied with 16 beds with
  • Timing of dialysis- 7:00 am to 3:00 pm
  • 16 machines. Two shifts. 4:00 hours per dialysis. 32 patients per day capacity.
  • 800 Dialysis cycles per month.

Ramzan And Monthly Ration Aid

Several families living on the edge of starvation are also supplied with monthly ration and relief in the form of annual Ramzan giving. Families that are battling for everyday survival are selected for this program. Backing the families of those patients who are on dialysis is also a part of this plan. The level of extreme poverty in India today is of 50 million people, which may come down to 40 million, which means the poverty rate getting declined by 3%, the Brookings report has said.


Reality Check

Monthly ration provision for poor families to break the chain of malnutrition.


Monthly Ritual

  • Identifying deprived families
  • Area-wide selection of Fair Price Shops
  • Periodic quality check at distribution centres
  • Determining item-wise utilization eligibility criteria for families
  • Informing families of their entitlements
  • Arrive at the shops on the fixed date to oversee distribution
  • Distribution of essential items
  • KCT field officer makes the final payment

WHY SHOULD ANYONE APPROACH KCT FOR HELP AND ASSISTANCE?

  • Medicines from the KCT are delivered to the patient’s doorstep the very same day.
  • It is ensured that the medicines strips are cut and the bottles are unsealed to avoid resale/misuse of medicines.
  • KCT has tie up with various chemists who provide high discounts on these medicines.
  • Patients’ applications are carefully and promptly scrutinized by the trustees before a cheque is issued, making sure that the treatment does not get unnecessarily delayed.
  • Around 150 to 180 patients receive KCT’s assistance, every day.
  • KCT is associated with several other trusts to arrange major expenses, say for a bypass surgery etc.
  • Distribution of essential items
  • Where needed, KCT also provides pre-surgery support as well as financial assistance to buy shoes,belt and surgical sutures.
  • All trustees regularly visit KCT office.
  • No references are sought and each patient is treated equally.
  • Refreshment and proper seating arrangement are provided to the patient and their relatives.
